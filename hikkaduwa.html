Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery

The sea turtle is an endangered species. The main reason for this is human activities. there is an illegal industry trading turtle eggs, shells and meat. There are also turtles that are injured by boats and fishing nets. Another factor that increases ocean pollution is quite harmful to the turtles.
A turtle Hatchery is the place where the sea turtles swim and return back to hatch their eggs. After that, the hatchery authorities take care of the hatched eggs untill they are ready to swim in the water.
However, there are organizations trying to improve the situation. Hikkaduwa turtle hatchery is one of them. Efforts are being made to restore the population and educate both locals and tourists alike.
The turtle eggs are being watched over, and eventually the baby turtles are released back into the sea when they are ready.
In the hatchery the injured turtles are followed by veterinarians. Some may be missing fins or have open wounds. Adult turtles are in the hatchery for rehabilitation before being released back into the ocean.

You may also be lucky enough to visit Hikkaduwa and see turtle babies hatch on the beach in Hikkaduwa.

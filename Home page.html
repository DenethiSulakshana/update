Today there are 250 species of Turtles on the earth. Seventh of these live in the ocean and are called 'Sea Turtles'. The remaining 243 species live in the land or freshwater ponds and marshes.

The sea Turtle called 'Kasbawa' in Sinhala.
Five of the world's seven species of sea Turtles come to Sri Lanka's shores to breed. They are,
  1. 01.The Green Sea Turtle
  2. 02.Hawksbill Sea Turtle
  3. 03.Olive Ridley Sea Turtle
  4. 04.Loggerhead Sea Turtle
  5. 05.Leatherback Sea Turtle

Turtle take at least 20 years to mature, sometimes even as much as 30 years. After mating at sea, mature females come ashore to lay their eggs. They always come after dark. The female moves as far up the coast as possible to lay her eggs. This is to ensure that the water that comes with the tide does not reach the eggs and spoil them.

The eggs take around 50 days to hatch and immediately after hatchiling, they are move to the sea. Crows, seagulls and other sea birds eat some of the black chicks as they make their way on the white sand.

The main breeding season is from September to Mid-April on the West coast of the island and from February to June on the East coast. During these periods there are no monsoons to moisten the eggs and also the beaches are very wide and sandy.

Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery

This is the oldest conservation and research progect started in Sri Lanka in early 1978. It was a time when all seven sea turtless species were on the endangered list.

Of seven species, five of the species were found to be frequenting the beaches of the southern coast of Sri Lanka where they went ashore, to lay their eggs. These eggs were collected by the villagers and sold in the open market.
